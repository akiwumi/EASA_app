# EASA_app vs Web Manuals for Flight Schools

> Last updated: 2026-05-01
> Purpose: Positioning, sales enablement, and product strategy

## Strategic View

Web Manuals is strong at aviation document control. It is credible, compliance-oriented, and well established. For flight schools, the opportunity is not to copy it exactly. The opportunity is to build a product that feels more native to ATO operations and pilot training.

## Category Framing

| Dimension | Web Manuals | EASA_app opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| Core identity | Document control and compliance platform | Training and compliance operating system |
| Best fit | Aviation operators with strong manual-control needs | Flight schools that need manuals, training delivery, and compliance together |
| Daily users | Compliance-heavy teams | Compliance teams, instructors, and students |
| User experience | Structured and controlled | Structured, but simpler and more role-aware |

## What Flight Schools Likely Like About Web Manuals

| Strength | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Revision control | Schools can track approved versions and avoid old document confusion |
| Compliance links | Useful for mapping regulations to internal manuals |
| Controlled distribution | Instructors and students can be pushed toward the latest material |
| Audit readiness | Approval and publication flows create traceability |
| Aviation credibility | Buyers trust a platform built for aviation documentation |

## Where Web Manuals Feels Vulnerable For ATOs

| Weakness | Why EASA_app can win |
|---|---|
| Feels document-first | Build training workflows into the product, not around it |
| Can feel heavy for smaller schools | Offer faster onboarding and clearer pricing |
| Collaboration can feel rigid | Add text-level comments, review threads, and clearer resolution states |
| Student experience is not the center of gravity | Make student reading and acknowledgement simple and modern |
| Enterprise packaging | Use school-friendly plans and shorter commitments |

## Product Comparison Table

| Capability | Web Manuals | EASA_app target |
|---|---|---|
| Manual revisions | Strong | Match |
| Approval workflow | Strong | Match with simpler defaults |
| Compliance mapping | Strong | Match, with plainer impact summaries |
| Student reading experience | Adequate | Better |
| Instructor workflows | Limited product emphasis | Better |
| Lesson-linked content | Not a core product story | Strong differentiator |
| Sign-offs and acknowledgements | Present in document context | Expand into training context |
| Onboarding from messy school docs | Can require more structure and training | Make import and cleanup a core strength |
| Pricing clarity for small ATOs | Moderate | Better |
| Daily usefulness outside compliance | Moderate | Better |

## Positioning Lines

### Short version

Web Manuals helps you control manuals. EASA_app helps you run training safely and stay compliant.

### Sales version

If your main problem is enterprise-grade manual control, Web Manuals is a strong benchmark. If your school needs manuals, student reading, instructor workflows, and EASA change tracking in one place, EASA_app is the more focused fit.

### Homepage version

Built for flight schools, not adapted from airline manuals software.

## Recommended Win Themes

- Faster to adopt
- Easier for students and instructors
- Better training workflow integration
- Simpler, clearer pricing
- More actionable EASA change impact

## Avoid In Sales

- Do not claim better enterprise breadth unless it is true
- Do not position only as a cheaper copy
- Do not over-index on AI without explainability and approval controls

## Best Narrative

The strongest story is that EASA_app brings together three things flight schools usually manage separately:

- manuals and revisions
- training delivery and reading
- EASA change awareness and compliance action
