PART A – THE TRAINING PLAN

A:1 The Aim of the course PPL(A)

The aim of this course is to train pilots to receive the theoretical and practical
knowledge to act as PIC on single engine airplanes engaged in non-commercial
operations.

A:2 Pre-Entry Requirements – PPL(A)


(i). Minimum age, educational requirements (including language), medical certificate
must be obtained before solo flying is permitted.

An applicant for PPL shall be at least 17 years age.

(ii). Any individual Member State Requirement

Not applicable

(iii). Required Knowledge of Mathematics, Physics and English

Before being admitted to the course the applicant must have a sufficient level of
knowledge of Mathematics, Physics and English to facilitate an understanding of the
content of the course. A passing grade in the subjects on High School level shall
suffice. In case the student does not possess such a grade, the school shall administer
a test to satisfy itself of the level of knowledge.

(iv). Conditions
Not applicable.

(v). Exception
None.

(vi). Training Course
Applicants for PPL(A) shall complete a training course. The course shall include
theoretical knowledge and flight instruction as of this manual.

(vii). Theoretical knowledge examination
Applicants shall demonstrate a level of theoretical knowledge, appropriate to the
privileges granted, through examinations in the following subjects:

Air law,                                                                          —

Communications,                                                                   —

A:3 Credits and previous experience

(a) Applicants for a PPL(A) shall have completed at least 45 hours of flight instruction
in airplanes or TMGs, 5 of which may have been completed in an FSTD, including at
least:
(1)25 hours of dual flight instruction; and
(2)10 hours of supervised solo flight time, including at least 5 hours of solo cross-
country flight time with at least 1 cross-country flight of at least 270 km (150 NM),
during which full stop landings at 2 aerodromes different from the aerodrome of
departure shall be made.

(b) Specific requirements for applicants holding an LAPL(A). Applicants for a
PPL(A) holding an LAPL(A) shall have completed at least 15 hours of flight time on
airplanes after the issue of the LAPL(A), of which at least 10 shall be flight
instruction completed in a training course at an ATO. This training course shall
include at least 4 hours of supervised solo flight time, including at least 2 hours of
solo cross-country flight time with at least 1 cross-country flight of at least 270 km
(150 NM), during which full stop landings at 2 aerodromes different from the
aerodrome of departure shall be made.

(c) Specific requirements for applicants holding an LAPL(S) with a TMG extension.
Applicants for a PPL(A) holding an LAPL(S) with a TMG extension shall have
completed:

(1) At least 24 hours of flight time on TMG after the endorsement of the TMG
extension; and

(2) 15 hours of flight instruction in airplanes in a training course at a DTO or at an
ATO, including at least the requirements of (a)(2).

(d) Crediting: Applicants holding a pilot license for another category of aircraft, with
the exception of balloons, shall be credited with 10 % of their total flight time as PIC
on such aircraft up to a maximum of 10 hours. The amount of credit given shall in any
case not include the requirements in (a)(2).

A:4 Training Syllabi

Instruction shall follow syllabus as far as practical.
Syllabus is based on a minimum time and lessons required. The HT or designee may
extend the time and/or number of lessons required to reach proficiency.

(i) Practical Flying Instruction:

All in accordance with appendix A1 of this manual.

(a) Applicants for a PPL(A) at SSFA shall have completed at least 45 hours of flight
instruction in airplanes, 5 of which may have been completed in an FSTD, including
at least:

(1) 35 hours of dual flight instruction; and

(2) 10 hours of supervised solo flight time, including at least 5 hours of solo cross-
country flight time with at least 1 cross-country flight of at least 270 km (150 NM),
during which full stop landings at 2 aerodromes different from the aerodrome of
departure shall be made.

The student shall have passed all flight lessons, passed all the end of phase progress
checks including the final check-ride to successfully complete this training course.


(ii) FSTD:

All in accordance with appendix A1 of this manual.
5 hours may be completed in an FSTD.


(iii) Theoretical Knowledge Instruction:

All in accordance with appendix B1 of this manual.

The theoretical course is 100 hours of instructor led lessons.

Student shall have adequate theoretical training in the systems, handling,
performance, normal procedures, and emergency procedures of the aircraft used in
training. This training is offered through aircraft specific technical courses and is a
natural part of briefing before and after a flying lesson.

A:9 Assessment, tests and examinations

(i) Flying:

(A) Progress checks:
A progress check is done twice per course as far as is practical. The progress check is
to be recorded in course documentation in Flightlogger under “Progress test Phase 1”
and “Progress test Phase 2” and completed by HT, CFI or designee. The progress
check is to evaluate the overall performance of the student.

(B) Skill tests:
Before presenting the student for a skill test, the school shall perform a Practical
Flying Test (Check ride) so to satisfy itself of the competence of the student.

The Practical Flying Test (check ride) may not be done before the applicant has
successfully passed the theoretical examinations and completed all the lessons of the
flight training.
An applicant for the skill test shall have received flight instruction on the same class
of aircraft to be used for the skill test.
The flying test (check ride) may be part of last session in the course.
The check ride shall be divided into different sections, representing all the different
phases of flight appropriate to the category of aircraft flown.
After passing the check ride, the student will be eligible for a skill test.

Applicants for a PPL shall demonstrate through the completion of a check ride the
ability to perform, as PIC on a single engine piston, the relevant procedures and
maneuvers with competency appropriate to the privileges granted.

Pass marks:
(1) Failure in any item of a section will cause the applicant to fail the entire section.

(2) Failure in more than 1 section will cause the applicant to fail the entire test. If the
applicant fails only 1 section, he/she shall at least repeat only that section.

(3) When the check ride needs to be repeated in accordance with (2), failure in any
section, including those that have been passed on a previous attempt, will cause the
applicant to fail the entire test.

(4) Failure to achieve a pass in all sections of the test in 2 attempts will require further
training.

(ii) Theoretical knowledge:

(A) Progress tests;
During theoretical instructions, the instructor shall orally quiz/question any or all
students as of their understanding of the subject taught as needed.
During theoretical instructions, the students shall be tested at least twice during each
of the subjects by a multiple choice written test. The tests is a progress test and a
mandatory test. Passing rate for the test shall be 80% of correct answers.

A student not passing a mandatory test shall be given opportunity for additional
training before retaking the test. In case a student fails three times in a subject, the
HT/DHT shall decide further actions.

(B) Theoretical knowledge examinations.
Theoretical examination shall be done in accordance with the Authorities directives.

Before presenting the student to Theoretical Examination, the school shall perform a
Mandatory Test so to satisfy itself of the competence of the student. Passing rate for
the test shall be 80%.

(C) Area 100 KSA assessment;
Not applicable.

(iii) Authorization for test:
Authorization for skill test and theory examinations shall be done by HT or designee.
Before authorization is granted for a theoretical knowledge examination, the student
shall have attended all the theory, passed the progress tests and the mandatory test.
Before authorization is granted for a skill test, the student shall have passed all theory
tests and examinations, completed all the practical flying instructions and passed the
check ride.

(iv) Rules concerning refresher training before retest;
If the student fails to achieve a passing grade in a test, a corrective action plan has to
be given and student may only be entered for re-examination after corrective action
plan has been completed.

(v) Test reports and records;
All tests shall be retained with the student’s records. Any test reports and records shall
be retained for a period of three years. All according to A:7 of this manual.

(vi)Procedures for examination paper preparation, type of questions and assessment,
standard required for “pass”;
All test is in either a paper or in digital form. All test have a passing rate of 80%. Test
questions shall be ”multiple choice”, be relevant for the subject and at representable
level.

B:3 Course structure: phase of training:

Instruction shall follow syllabus as far as practical.
Syllabus is based on a minimum time and lessons required. The HT/CFI may extend
the time and/or number of lessons required to reach proficiency.

All in accordance with appendix A1 of this manual.

Applicants for a PPL(A) shall have completed at least 45 hours of flight instruction in
airplanes, 5 of which may have been completed in an FSTD, including at least:

35 of dual flight instruction; and

10:00 of supervised solo flight time, including at least 5 hours of solo cross-country
flight time with at least 1 cross-country flight of at least 270 km (150 NM), during
which full stop landings at 2 aerodromes different from the aerodrome of departure
shall be made.

The practical flying training is divided in 3 phases:
1-First solo preparation, lesson 1-13
2-First long solo navigation preparation, lesson 14-34
3-Precision and skill test preparation, lesson 35-42

A progress check is done twice per course as far as is practical. The progress check is
to be recorded in course documentation in Flightlogger under “Progress test Phase 1”
and “Progress test Phase 2” and completed by HT, CFI or designee. The progress
check is to evaluate the overall performance of the student.
Emergency drills are incorporated in every lesson.
Lessons are to be repeated until passed.
